Banking in Colombia

The best bank for ever

Our history is closely linked to the history of the country. We have worked alongside Colombians, businessmen, women, youth and children who believe in building a better country.

  • 1870 - Banco de Bogotá began work on November 15 as the first financial institution created in the country.
  • 1876 - Overcoming the crisis - The Bank overcomes the crisis of the Government of the time.
  • 1899 - Thousand days war - The Bank also overcomes the crisis of the Thousand Day War, which torpedoed the normal development of the national banking system.
  • 1922 - First branch of Banco de Bogotá in the city of Girardot The entity was a pioneer in establishing a branch system, opening an office in Girardot (Cundinamarca).
  • 1960-Aerobank implementation - The entity presented the Aerobanco, a service aimed at solving communication and cash transportation difficulties of the country's population.
  • 1968 - Merger with Banco de los Andes - The merger with Banco de los Andes becomes effective.
  • 1969 - Credibanco System - The Bank brought the credit card system to the country, entering the market under the name of Credibanco, as a Bank of America concessionaire.
  • 1974 - New York Office - The entity established an office in New York, an operation that was complemented in 1977 with the creation of the Banco de Bogotá Trust Company, which was later called First Bank of the Americas.
  • 1992 - Diversification of services - With the enthusiasm of the creation of a new constitution and economic opening, during this decade the acquisition and merger with Banco del Comercio was completed. Likewise, fidubogotá, Leasing Porvernir and the Porvenir Pension Fund are established, with financial solutions for all Colombians.
  • 2006 - The merger with Megabanco - Having an offer adapted to micro and small businesses, and fostering the consolidation of innovative social banking solutions led to the merger with Megabanco, which by then was an active promoter of the category. With this acquisition, Banco de Bogotá complemented its offer as a universal bank and advanced in its purpose of bankarization and incursion in the money order market, vehicle credit, social banking and microcredit.
  • 2011 - First bond issue in the international market - US $ 600 million was the result of the first bond issue that Banco de Bogotá made in the international market. A figure that reflects the clear confidence that international analysts have in the entity.
  • 2013 - Environmental commitment - The Banco de Bogotá, aware of the environmental problem, created the Green Credit Line in alliance with the Bank of Japan to finance initiatives aimed at the prevention, control and mitigation of the effects of CO2 emissions. Ratifying its commitment to environmental sustainability, the entity also signed the Green Protocol agreement, signed by the government and the Colombian financial sector, to work for the proper use of natural resources and environmental preservation.
